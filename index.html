About Me

I am a Hardware Design Engineer with 5+ years of experience delivering production-ready analog, power, and mixed-signal PCB designs across consumer electronics and automotive systems. My work spans full ownership of the hardware lifecycle—from schematic architecture and component selection to PCB layout, manufacturing release, and board bring-up.

For Project Moonstone, I bring hands-on KiCad experience and a delivery-first approach: clean schematics, DFM-aware layouts, complete manufacturing outputs (Gerbers, drill, BOM, pick-and-place), and disciplined debug infrastructure (test points, indicators) to accelerate bring-up.

HeatKept Power PCB

Consumer Power & Control PCB — HeatKept LLC

  • Designed high-current power path for heater control (~1.8–2A at 18–28V) using MCU-driven PWM.
  • Integrated USB-C Power Delivery controller for dynamic voltage negotiation.
  • Architected multi-rail power tree (21–28V → 5V → 3.3V) with buck and LDO regulation.
  • Applied layout best practices for tight switching loops, decoupling placement, and thermal copper distribution.
  • Added debug infrastructure including test points and LED indicators to accelerate bring-up.
  • Validated design under load transients and supported hardware bring-up.
  • Representative PCB layout images and bring-up summary available via linked repository.

Green Tiger Automotive PCB

Automotive VCU & IoT Telemetry Platform — Green Tiger Mobility

  • Owned end-to-end schematic design for Vehicle Control Units and IoT telemetry boards.
  • Integrated ESP32 MCU, GPS, GSM, and 4G modules using UART and I²C interfaces.
  • Designed automotive-grade power electronics handling 12–60V rails with PMOS/NMOS switching and optocoupler isolation.
  • Executed multi-layer PCB layouts with attention to power integrity, return paths, and EMI robustness.
  • Generated full manufacturing release: Gerbers, drill files, BOM, pick-and-place, and fab notes.
  • Supported board bring-up and debug of communication and power subsystems.
